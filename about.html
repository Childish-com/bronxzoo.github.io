About The Bronx Zoo

The Bronx Zoo is a zoo located within Bronx Park in the Bronx, a borough of New York City. It is one of the largest zoos in the United States by area, and is the largest metropolitan zoo in the United States by area, comprising 265 acres (107 ha) of park lands and naturalistic habitats. The zoo is part of the Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS).

History

The Bronx Zoo opened its doors on November 8, 1899, making it the largest zoo in the United States at the time. It was founded by the New York Zoological Society, now known as the Wildlife Conservation Society. Over the years, it has evolved from a traditional zoo to a modern conservation park, focusing on wildlife preservation and education.

Key Attractions

  • Congo Gorilla Forest: A 6.5-acre habitat for gorillas and other African wildlife.
  • Tiger Mountain: Home to Siberian tigers in a Himalayan-inspired landscape.
  • Madagascar!: An exhibit featuring lemurs, fossas, and other Madagascar natives.
  • Butterfly Garden: A seasonal exhibit with thousands of butterflies.
  • African Plains: Savanna habitat for zebras, giraffes, and antelopes.
